CSR Estimates for UH FEL Transport Line Chicanes
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

Beam parameters:
  E = 40 MeV (γ = 79.3, β ≈ 1.0)
  Q = 60 pC  (N_b = 3.75 × 10⁸)
  ε_n = 8 π·mm·mrad
  σ_t = 2 ps  → σ_z = 600 μm, I_peak = 12.0 A
  σ_t = 0.5 ps → σ_z = 150 μm, I_peak = 47.9 A

Chicane geometry (FC1, final chicane before undulator):
  4-dipole symmetric chicane: +θ, -θ, -θ, +θ
  θ = 11.25° per dipole
  L_d = 37.4 mm (magnetic length)
  ρ = 190 mm (bending radius)
  Drift spacing: 329 mm / 191 mm / 329 mm (center-to-center)
  R56 ≈ 2.9 mm

Upstream chicanes (×4, smaller angles):
  θ = 4° per dipole, L_d = 40.6 mm, ρ = 581 mm (weaker bending)

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
Steady-state CSR (Derbenev–Saldin formula)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

Formula: ΔE_CSR = 0.22 × r_e × m_e c² × N_b × L_d / (ρ^{2/3} × σ_z^{4/3})

FC1 chicane (4 dipoles, θ = 11.25°, ρ = 190 mm):

  σ_t = 2 ps (baseline):
    ΔE_CSR = 4 × 0.68 keV = 2.7 keV  (per chicane)
    σ_δ,CSR = 0.007%
    Δε/ε = 0.0002% (negligible)

  σ_t = 0.5 ps (compressed):
    ΔE_CSR = 4 × 6.8 keV = 27 keV  (per chicane)
    σ_δ,CSR = 0.068%
    Δε/ε = 0.02%

Upstream chicanes (θ = 4°, ρ = 581 mm, ×4 chicanes):
  CSR ~3× weaker per dipole (larger ρ), but cumulative across 4 chicanes.
  Total upstream: ~0.5× FC1 contribution.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
Impact assessment
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

                            2 ps        0.5 ps
  Base σ_δ               0.50%        0.50%
  CSR σ_δ (FC1)          0.007%       0.068%
  CSR / base             1.4%         14%
  Emittance growth       negligible   0.02%

Conclusion:

At the nominal 2 ps bunch length, CSR is negligible — the induced energy
spread (0.007%) is two orders of magnitude below the beam's intrinsic
σ_δ = 0.5%, and the emittance growth is < 0.001%.

At 0.5 ps (compressed), CSR becomes measurable but remains small: σ_δ,CSR
contributes ~14% relative to the base energy spread. The CSR-induced
emittance growth (0.02%) is still negligible for FEL operation.

The dominant collective effect is CSR in the FC1 chicane (shortest ρ).
LSC and resistive-wall wakefields are subdominant by 1-2 orders of
magnitude (see S9_bunch_length_study.py for full comparison).

Note: These are steady-state estimates. Transient CSR effects (entrance/exit
of dipoles) can produce ~2× larger instantaneous energy kicks but average
out over the chicane. A full numerical CSR simulation (e.g., ELEGANT or
CSRtrack) would be needed for precision below ~10%.
