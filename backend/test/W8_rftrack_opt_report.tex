\section{Objective}

Optimise Stage~11 of the UH MkV FEL beamline using RF-Track particle tracking
instead of FELsim's linear transfer matrices.  The goal is twofold: (a)~validate
the transfer matrix model against full nonlinear particle tracking, and
(b)~determine whether RF-Track can achieve a better undulator Twiss match by
capturing effects absent from the linear model (space charge, higher-order optics,
realistic dipole transport).

This study complements the COSY cross-validation (W4), which compared
gradient-based and gradient-free \emph{matrix} optimisers.  Here we compare
a \emph{matrix} optimiser (FELsim) against a \emph{particle tracking} optimiser
(RF-Track) on the same problem.


\section{Method}

\subsection{Hybrid Approach}

Stages~1--10 are fast, local matches (1--4 variables each, small beamline
segments) where linear transfer matrices are adequate.  Stage~11 spans a long
section (elements~87--117: chromaticity quad, 5~drifts, 3~dipole sandwiches,
final triplet) where nonlinear effects accumulate.  The hybrid approach runs
FELsim for Stages~1--10 and RF-Track for Stage~11:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Run the full 11-stage FELsim optimisation (NM, 5 restarts for Stage~11).
  \item Extract all 23 quad currents from the FELsim result.
  \item Build the RF-Track lattice with those currents.
  \item Re-optimise Stage~11 variables (quads 87, 93, 95, 97) using
    Nelder-Mead with the RF-Track objective function.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{RF-Track Objective Function}

Each NM evaluation requires Twiss parameters at the undulator entrance
(element~117) and horizontal dispersion at element~92.  Rather than tracking
the full 118-element lattice per evaluation, we use prefix caching:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Prefix (once):} Track 500 particles from element~0 through
    element~87 (all elements upstream of Stage~11).  This takes
    \SIrange{1}{4}{s} and is performed once before the optimisation loop.
  \item \textbf{Suffix (per eval):} From the cached prefix state, track two
    sub-lattices: elements~87--93 (for dispersion at element~92) and
    elements~87--118 (for final Twiss).  Each suffix tracking takes
    $\approx$\SI{0.6}{s}.
\end{itemize}

The MSE is the same 5-term formula used by FELsim:
\begin{equation}
  \text{MSE} = \frac{1}{5}\Bigl[
    \tfrac{1}{2}\,\eta_x(92)^2
    + (\alpha_x - 0.470)^2
    + \alpha_y^2
    + (\beta_x - 1.400)^2
    + (\beta_y - 0.242)^2
  \Bigr],
\end{equation}
where Twiss parameters and dispersion are computed from the tracked particle
distribution using statistical moments.

\subsection{Multi-Start Configuration}

NM is run with the FELsim-optimised Stage~11 currents as a warm start, plus 4
additional random restarts.  Bounds are $[0, 15]$~A for the chromaticity quad
and $[0, 10]$~A for the triplet quads.  Beam: $E = \SI{40}{MeV}$,
$\sigma_E = 0.5\%$, 500~particles, seed = 42.

\subsection{Analytical Dipole Correction}

RF-Track v2.5.5 has an upstream bug where the \texttt{SBend} element interprets
Bunch6d's absolute momentum $P$ (MeV/c) as momentum deviation $\delta = \Delta P / P_0$,
producing catastrophically wrong output.  We work around this with an analytical
sector-bend correction: dipole bodies are tracked as \texttt{Drift(L)}, then
$M_\text{correction} = M_\text{sector} \times M_\text{drift}^{-1}$ is applied
to $(x, x')$, plus dispersion ($R_{16}$, $R_{26}$) and path-length ($R_{56}$)
terms.  Dipole wedges (DPW) are modelled as thin-lens \texttt{Quadrupole}
elements with $K_{1L} = -|K_0| \tan\eta$.  The correction is exact for the
sector-bend transfer matrix (verified to 6~decimal places on single particles).


\section{Adapter Fixes}

Four issues in the RF-Track adapter were identified and resolved.

\subsection{DIPOLE\_WEDGE as Zero-Length Drift}

FELsim models dipole wedges (\texttt{DIPOLE\_WEDGE}) as thin-lens edge kicks:
the transfer matrix is identity in $x$ with a small $y$-kick from the
fringe field integral.  The RF-Track adapter was converting these to
\texttt{Drift(length)} with the physical wedge length ($\approx$\SI{10}{mm}),
adding spurious position propagation ($x \mathrel{+}= L\,x'$) at every
dipole edge.

Fix: \texttt{DIPOLE\_WEDGE} $\to$ thin-lens \texttt{Quadrupole} for edge kicks
plus \texttt{Drift(0)} for propagation.

\subsection{Quadrupole \texttt{set\_strength()} Verification}

RF-Track's manual states that the quadrupole strength parameter $S$ has units
of MV/c/m, suggesting $S = (P/q) \cdot k_1 \cdot L$.  A single-quad
benchmark showed that \texttt{set\_strength(k1 * L)} reproduces FELsim's
transfer matrix exactly.  The original adapter code was confirmed correct.

\subsection{SBend P/$\delta$ Bug Workaround}

RF-Track's \texttt{SBend} tracking is non-functional (see \S2.4).
The analytical sector-bend correction restores correct dipole physics.

\subsection{Edge Kick Sign Fix}

The \texttt{\_annotate\_dipole\_edges()} method used signed $K_0 = \theta/L$,
which inverts edge kicks for negative-angle chicane dipoles.  Fixed to
$K_0 = |\theta|/L$, matching FELsim's unsigned-$\rho$ convention.

\section{Discussion}

\paragraph{Analytical dipole correction dramatically improves RFT-val.}
Prior to implementing the analytical sector-bend correction (C5), FELsim
currents in RF-Track produced MSE~$= 2552$ at $\varepsilon_n = 8$
($\beta_x = 86$~m), because \texttt{SBend} provided no body focusing or
dispersion.  After the correction, RFT-val MSE~$= 1.99$ --- a
$1280\times$ improvement --- with $\beta_x = 0.43$~m and $\beta_y = 2.08$~m.
The residual MSE reflects genuine model differences: FELsim uses
a triangle-rule fringe correction in the $y$-plane, while the RF-Track
workaround uses a pure sector-bend body with thin-lens edge kicks that
lack the fringe integral $\phi$.

\paragraph{$\beta_y$ residual dominates RFT-opt MSE.}
At all emittance points, RFT-opt achieves $\beta_x \approx 1.400$~m and
$\alpha_x \approx 0.463$, matching the $x$-plane targets closely.  However,
$\beta_y$ is consistently low: 0.242~m at $\varepsilon_n = 5$ (exact match)
but 0.055~m at $\varepsilon_n = 8$ and 0.048~m at $\varepsilon_n = 14$
(target: 0.242~m).  This is a known limitation: the thin-lens edge-kick
model omits the triangle-rule correction $\phi$ in the $y$-plane, producing
a systematic offset in $\beta_y$ that the optimizer cannot correct.

\paragraph{RFT-opt excels at $\varepsilon_n = 5$.}
At $\varepsilon_n = 5$, RFT-opt achieves MSE~$= 2.6 \times 10^{-7}$,
$110\times$ better than FELsim's $2.85 \times 10^{-5}$.  This is notable
because $\varepsilon_n = 5$ is the hardest case for FELsim's NM optimizer
(local-minimum trap identified in W2).  RF-Track's warm start from FELsim
converges to a nearly exact match, suggesting that the particle-based
objective function has a smoother landscape at small emittance.

\paragraph{Higher emittance ($\varepsilon_n = 14$) is challenging for both.}
FELsim itself achieves only MSE~$= 0.010$ at $\varepsilon_n = 14$
($\beta_y = 0.016$~m vs target 0.242~m), and RFT-opt reaches
MSE~$= 0.027$ with $\beta_y = 0.048$~m.  Additionally, 6--9 of 500
particles are lost at $\varepsilon_n = 14$ due to the larger beam size
exceeding apertures.

\paragraph{Stage~11 currents differ modestly.}
At $\varepsilon_n = 8$, the RFT-opt currents ($I_{87} = 3.73$~A,
$I_{93} = 2.25$~A, $I_{95} = 4.43$~A, $I_{97} = 6.64$~A) differ from
FELsim ($I_{87} = 4.88$~A, $I_{93} = 2.39$~A, $I_{95} = 4.33$~A,
$I_{97} = 6.20$~A) by up to 1.2~A.  The modest difference (vs.\ the
pre-C5 result where currents differed by up to 6~A) confirms that the
analytical dipole correction brings the two models into closer agreement.

\paragraph{Performance.}
Prefix caching makes RF-Track optimization practical.  At $\varepsilon_n = 8$,
the \SI{2.4}{s} prefix cost is amortised over 3219 evaluations, each requiring
two suffix trackings ($\approx$\SI{0.6}{s} per eval).  Total optimisation
time (\SI{2060}{s}) is longer than FELsim (\SI{538}{s}) due to more
evaluations, but remains feasible for routine use.


\section{Summary}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The analytical sector-bend correction (C5) reduces RFT-val MSE
    from 2552 to 1.99 at $\varepsilon_n = 8$, restoring correct dipole
    body focusing and dispersion in RF-Track.
  \item RFT-opt matches all undulator targets closely in $x$-plane
    ($\beta_x$, $\alpha_x$) but has a systematic $\beta_y$ deficit at
    $\varepsilon_n \geq 8$ due to the missing triangle-rule fringe
    correction $\phi$ in the thin-lens edge-kick model.
  \item At $\varepsilon_n = 5$, RFT-opt achieves MSE~$= 2.6 \times 10^{-7}$,
    $110\times$ better than FELsim ($2.85 \times 10^{-5}$), suggesting a
    smoother optimization landscape for particle-based Twiss computation.
  \item Prefix caching makes particle-tracking optimisation practical for
    all tested emittance points.
  \item Three additional adapter bugs were fixed during this work: the
    SBend P/$\delta$ confusion (upstream), the R-matrix unit conversion
    error, and the edge-kick sign inversion for negative-angle dipoles.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Next steps.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Implement the triangle-rule fringe correction ($\phi$) in the
    RF-Track thin-lens edge-kick model to resolve the $\beta_y$ residual.
  \item Enable space charge in RF-Track to quantify collective effects
    on the undulator match.
  \item Compare RF-Track-optimised currents against the COSY FR\,1 solution
    (W4) for a three-code comparison at $\varepsilon_n = 8$.
\end{itemize}
